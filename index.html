About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Scholar Researcher in the Department of Civil, Construction, and Environmental Engineering at North Carolina State University, where I work at the intersection of pavement engineering and transportation safety.

My research focuses on improving how engineers manage pavements' structural and frictional performance. This management process has long-lasting impacts on the total transportation system and its economic, environmental, and social dimensions.

My central research goal is to find a quantifiable relationship between the most common pavement condition indices used in Pavement Management Systems (PMS) and highway safety outcomes — ultimately integrating safety performance into PMS decision-making.

Focus Areas

Research

Active
01

Pavement–Safety Integration

Developing predictive models that link pavement surface condition indices (IRI, friction, rutting) to crash frequency and severity, enabling safety-informed PMS decisions.

Active
02

Frictional Performance Monitoring

Characterizing pavement skid resistance degradation over time and its relationship to wet-weather crash rates across the NCDOT highway network.

Active
03

GIS-Based Spatial Analysis

Applying geospatial tools to overlay pavement condition data, crash records, and traffic volume across large statewide networks to identify high-risk corridors.

04

Structural Performance Modeling

Quantifying the structural health of flexible pavements using deflection data and relating structural deficiency to increased safety risk for road users.

05

PMS Decision-Making

Evaluating treatment selection frameworks in state DOT Pavement Management Systems to incorporate multi-objective optimization including safety outcomes.

06

Environmental & Equity Impacts

Assessing the social and environmental dimensions of pavement deterioration, including disproportionate impacts on rural and underserved communities.
